## Thesis
E-commerce is not one business. Founders win when product type, trust mechanism, and channel economics line up from day one.

## Summary
A ground-level map of how Indian commerce actually moves across marketplaces, social selling, wholesale pipes, and demand density outside metros.

## Audience fit
Early-stage founders choosing between marketplace, social commerce, B2B, and owned-store motions.

## Framework
1. Choose products with visible utility or natural repeat before chasing broad catalog depth.
2. Separate discovery-led products from replenishment-led products and route them through the right channel.
3. Use marketplaces and social commerce to map demand, then build owned retention and wholesale depth around proven demand pockets.
4. Treat working capital, supply reliability, and merchandising as frontline growth levers, not finance-only concerns.

## High-signal takeaways
- Channel-product fit beats generic omnichannel talk: The smartest path is not 'sell everywhere' but matching the right product to the channel that naturally explains, demonstrates, and replenishes it.
- Assisted trust still matters outside top metros: Tier-2 and Tier-3 demand exists, but assisted buying and trust transfer remain decisive in many categories.
- Distribution depth compounds faster than ad-led breadth: Once a category shows repeat demand, reliable supply and better shelf presence often outperform endless customer acquisition spending.

## Market white-space
Vernacular-first assisted commerce infrastructure that helps emerging Indian brands bridge social discovery, retailer networks, and repeat purchase outside metro bubbles.

## Resource stack
- Meesho: Signal marketplace for discovering value-sensitive demand pockets and social selling behavior. (https://www.meesho.com/)
- Udaan: B2B distribution benchmark for retailer access, assortment planning, and wholesale logistics. (https://www.udaan.com/)
- Marketplace search analytics: Use search terms and listing interaction data to understand what Indian customers actively hunt for.
