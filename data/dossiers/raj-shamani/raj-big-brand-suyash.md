## Thesis
Modern Indian brands win by obsessing over underserved product truth, review-led insight mining, and premium perception that feels deserved.

## Summary
Suyash Saraf distills how Dot & Key approached quality gaps, contract manufacturing, packaging, pricing, first customers, and branded search in Indian beauty.

## Audience fit
Founders building premium consumer brands for Indian digital natives.

## Framework
1. Pick categories where Indian consumers are still underserved on quality, localization, or usability.
2. Mine reviews, search behavior, and complaints until the missing benefit becomes impossible to ignore.
3. Lead with a hero product and premium packaging strong enough to signal trust instantly.
4. Use authority creators and performance distribution to build branded search, then expand into adjacent products.

## High-signal takeaways
- Review mining is product research: Consumer reviews reveal why products sell, why they fail, and where the strongest unmet need actually sits.
- Premium packaging is a conversion tool: The pack helps customers understand why this brand deserves the premium, especially in cluttered categories.
- Hero SKU focus creates momentum: A sharp first product makes marketing more efficient and gives the brand a story customers can repeat.

## Market white-space
Localized body care and premium beauty subcategories for Indian conditions, especially where existing options feel generic, imported, or poorly explained.

## Resource stack
- Google Trends: Validate whether consumer curiosity is emerging before you overbuild the category. (https://trends.google.com/)
- Marketplace reviews: Primary source for unmet need mining and product language customers already use.
- Contract manufacturer scorecard: Select for integrity, formulation consistency, and category depth, not just low cost.
